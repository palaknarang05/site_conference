About The International Conference (ICHBH-24)

Rationale of Conference

Humanities is a vast and diverse field of study that encompasses various aspects of human culture, language, literature, and arts. It explores the profound and complex questions about the human experience, history, values, and the world we inhabit. Beyond the Humanities: A Critical Approach to Language, Literature, and Cultural Studies goes beyond traditional boundaries to analyze and interpret these subjects from a critical perspective.

Objectives

This event seeks to foster interdisciplinary dialogue and critical analysis, exploring the intersections of humanities with language, literature, media, and management studies. It presents a unique opportunity to engage with emerging trends and research, as well as to contribute to shaping the future of these dynamic fields.

Organizers

The conference is organized by School of Advanced Sciences and Languages (SASL), VIT Bhopal University, Madhya Pradesh, India, in collaboration with AAB College, Pristina, Republic of Kosova, academic partner Emerald Publishing, media partner Conference Alert.

Call for Papers

We warmly invite scholars, researchers, practitioners, and students to submit their papers and participate in this enriching academic exchange. The conference will include keynote speeches, panel discussions, and paper presentations on a wide range of topics related to the theme.

ABOUT SCHOOL OF ADVANCED SCIENCES AND LANGUAGES(SASL)
The School of Advanced Sciences and Languages (SASL) in VIT Bhopal is principally concerned with blending the best knowledge of science and humanities with Engineering to align the expertise of our students with the ever changing technological requirements. The school offers numerous core and elective courses, developed in collaboration with international academia and industrial experts, in Mathematics, Chemistry, Physics, English and Humanities. The school imparts knowledge by employing student centered teaching approaches via state-of-the-art studio classrooms equipped with wave 2.0 technology. ABOUT AAB COLLEGE
AAB College is the largest non-public institution of higher education in the Albanian territory. AAB was founded in 2000 in Pristina. AAB is the first non-public institution of higher education in Kosovo which was established immediately after the war in Kosovo, after the creation of new economic-social and cultural circumstances, when conditions were created for the normal life of the citizens of Kosovo, breaking thus the conditions of a long monopoly in the field of education in our country. In its beginnings, our institution offered study programs in the field of Art and Mass Communication, constantly expanding with study programs in other fields, thus becoming a relevant academic center in the country and region and serving as a model institution of higher education not only in Kosovo, but also in the region. As a non-public institution, AAB College provided a genuine example of the promotion of institutional autonomy and academic freedom in teaching and scientific research within the institution. AAB is the largest investment in Kosovo in the field of education. In the University Campus in Pristina, there are 6 facilities dedicated to the exercise of educational activity, equipped with modern facilities for creative learning and contemporary technology. In addition to the sports recreation center that includes a swimming pool, football, volleyball, and tennis fields, AAB College also provides a sports gym, promoting the development of students’ social and athletic life. AAB has branches in Ferizaj and Gjakov with the same academic and infrastructural standards as the central campus in Pristina. AAB has passed accreditation and evaluation processes multiple times and offers programs accredited by external experts in fields like Economics, Law, Mass Communication, Arts, Social Sciences, and English at the Bachelor and Master levels. AAB College became a member of the Magna Charta Universitatum in 2010, ensuring recognition of university values and promoting cooperation with over 750 other universities. As the largest non-public institution of higher education in Kosovo, AAB continues to expand research capacities, providing a strong basis for the opening of doctoral studies.
